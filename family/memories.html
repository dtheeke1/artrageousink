December 1998 • Holiday

The Christmas Blizzard

Nobody could make it to Grandma’s house for Christmas dinner. So we made forts in the living room, played cards until 2am, and made popcorn on the stove. Turned out to be the best Christmas we ever had.

Summer 2004 • Vacation

Door County Camping Trip

The tent collapsed in the middle of the night. Everyone ended up sleeping in the minivan. We still talk about waking up to a deer staring through the windshield at 5am.

Shared by the Kids • 18 ❤

June 2012 • Milestone

The Graduation Party

Grandpa gave a speech that made everyone cry and laugh at the same time. The potato salad was somehow both too salty and too bland. We danced on the deck until the neighbors knocked.

Shared by Mom • 22 ❤

Thanksgiving 2009 • Holiday

The Turkey That Wasn’t

Forgot to thaw the turkey. We ordered pizza. Three different relatives claimed to have a backup plan. None of them actually had a backup plan. We ate at 9pm. 10 out of 10, would do again.

Shared by Aunt Barb • 31 ❤

August 2016 • Everyday

Sunday Morning Music

Dad made pancakes and put on the record player. We didn’t go anywhere, didn’t do anything special. Just a quiet Sunday that nobody wanted to end. We all ended up staying until dinner.

Shared by Everyone • 27 ❤

July 4th, 2001 • Holiday

The Firecracker Incident

Nobody is allowed to talk about what happened to the lawn chair. But everyone agrees it was the funniest Fourth of July we ever had, and Uncle Gary has never been allowed to bring fireworks again.

Shared by David • 40 ❤

March 2019 • Milestone

Grandma’s 80th

She said she didn’t want a party. She got a party. She cried happy tears at the doorway. Then she complained that we didn’t bring enough napkins. Then she danced. It was perfect.

Shared by the Family • 55 ❤

Fall 2007 • Kids

The Soccer Season

They lost every game. Every single one. But nobody cared. We had juice boxes and orange slices and cheered so loud for every kick that actually connected. The trophies were enormous. We still have them.

Shared by Mom • 19 ❤

Easter 2014 • Funny

The Dog Found the Easter Eggs First

We hid 48 Easter eggs in the backyard. The dog found 46 of them before the kids even went outside. The two remaining eggs weren’t discovered until June, when we mowed the lawn.

Shared by Dad • 44 ❤
